Aloo Paratha

Aloo Paratha is one of India's most beloved comfort foods, enjoyed from the bustling streets of Delhi to quiet family breakfast tables across the country. This delicious flatbread consists of a whole wheat dough stuffed with a savory, spiced potato mixture. It's then pan-fried, traditionally with ghee or oil, until it's golden brown and slightly crisp on the outside, while remaining soft and flavorful on the inside. Perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a hearty snack, the aloo paratha is a simple yet satisfying dish that warms the heart and soul.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • Whole Wheat Flour (Atta): 2 cups
  • Salt: ½ teaspoon
  • Water: Approximately 1 cup, warm (or as needed to knead)
  • Oil: 1 teaspoon (optional, for a softer dough)

For the Potato Filling:

  • Potatoes: 3-4 medium-sized, boiled and peeled
  • Onion: 1 small, finely chopped
  • Green Chillies: 1-2, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Coriander Leaves: 2 tablespoons, freshly chopped
  • Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder): 1 teaspoon
  • Garam Masala: ½ teaspoon
  • Red Chili Powder: ½ teaspoon (optional, adjust to taste)
  • Dry Mango Powder (Amchur): ½ teaspoon (for a tangy flavour, optional)
  • Salt: To taste

For Cooking:

  • Ghee or Oil: As needed for pan-frying the parathas

Directions

  1. Prepare the Dough

    1. In a mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of whole wheat flour (atta) with a pinch of salt.
    2. Slowly add warm water, a little at a time, and knead to form a soft and smooth dough.
    3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

  2. Make the Potato Filling

    1. Boil 3-4 medium-sized potatoes until they are soft. Peel them and mash them in a bowl.
    2. Add finely chopped onions, green chillies, and coriander leaves to the mashed potatoes.
    3. Mix in spices like red chili powder, garam masala, cumin powder, and salt to taste. Combine everything well.

  3. Assemble and Cook the Paratha

    1. Make small, lemon-sized balls from the dough.
    2. Flatten a dough ball with your fingers to form a small circle and place a spoonful of the potato filling in the center.
    3. Bring the edges of the dough together to seal the filling inside, forming a ball again.
    4. Lightly flour the stuffed ball and gently roll it out into a 6-7 inch circle.
    5. Heat a flat pan (tawa) on medium heat. Place the paratha on the hot tawa.
    6. Cook for about a minute until small bubbles appear, then flip it.
    7. Drizzle a little oil or ghee on the top side and spread it evenly. Flip again.
    8. Apply oil or ghee on the other side. Press gently with a spatula and cook until both sides are golden brown and crisp.
    9. Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or butter!
